0: Plants with medicinal or aromatic properties that are used in pharmacy and/or perfumery are usually defined as medicinal and aromatic plants; however, medicinal, aromatic and cosmetic plants would be a better term as many medicinal and aromatic plants are also used in cosmetics [ 1].
1: Aromatic plants are those that contain aromatic compounds  basically essential oils that are volatile at room temperature.
2: These essential oils are odorous, volatile, hydrophobic and highly concentrated compounds.
3: They can be obtained from flowers, buds, seeds, leaves, twigs, bark, wood, fruits and roots [ 2].
4: Essential oils are complex mixtures of secondary metabolites comprised low-boiling-point phenylpropenes and terpenes [ 3].
5: These oils usually consist of a about tens-to-hundreds of low molecular weight terpenoids.
6: Even unidentified trace constituents may be held responsible for altering the odour, flavour and the bioactivity of the oil to a considerable degree.
7: Essential oils have characteristic flavor and fragrance properties, possess biological activities and are widely applied in aromatherapy and healthcare in addition to several industries such as cosmetics, flavoring and fragrance, spices, pesticides and repellents, as well as herbal beverages.
