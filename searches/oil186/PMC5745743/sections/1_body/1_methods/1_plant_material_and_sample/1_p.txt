0: Fresh flowers of  O. fragrans var.
1: thunbergii were collected from the campus of Baoji University of Art and Science (10618 E, 3335N, 618 m altitude), Baoji, China, in late September, 2015.
2: The plant species was identified and confirmed by Prof. Xiaomei Wang, Shaanxi Key Laboratory of Phytochemistry, Baoji University of Art and Sciences.
3: The flowers (BJWLXY-CC-SKLP150915) were dried in the shade at 25 C until the humidity lower than 30%, and conserved in an airtight polyethylene bag at 20 C. Samples (10 g) were extracted by steam distillation.
4: The distilled liquid was then extracted with  n-hexane, and dehydrated with anhydrous magnesium sulfate, followed by evaporation.
5: Residual essential oil was stored at 20 C until use.
