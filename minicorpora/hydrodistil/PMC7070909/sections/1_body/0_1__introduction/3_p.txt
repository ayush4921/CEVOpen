0: The genus  Myrcia DC is the most representative of the Myrtaceae family.
1: In Brazil, it is represented by 23 genera and 974 species [ 5].
2: Many species of  Myrcia, such as  M. Silvatica,  M. punicifolia, and  M. speciosa are used in folk medicine, usually as infusions, for treating diabetes [ 6, 7].
3: Others, such as  M. salicifolia and  M. ovata are used in the treatment of gastric diseases, diarrhea, cold sores, and mouth ulcers [ 6, 8].
4: In addition, plants of the  Myrcia species are sources of EOs with antibacterial, antinociceptive, and anti-inflammatory activities [ 9, 10].
5: Sesquiterpenes and monoterpenes are the most frequently found components of their EOs [ 6].
