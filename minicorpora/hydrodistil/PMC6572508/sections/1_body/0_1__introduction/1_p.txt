1: Chrysopogon zizanioides (L.) Roberty, is a perennial plant of the  Poaceae family (subfamily  Panicoideae), native from India, and widely found in Asia, Africa, Oceania, Central, and South America [ 1].
2: The volatile oil from the vetiver root is a viscous liquid, with diverse colors from pale-yellow to dark brown, as well as a deep, smoky, earthy, and woody odor [ 2].
3: Vetiver oil is commonly used as a main odor contributor in the perfumery industry and as a flavor agent in the food industry [ 1, 3].
4: Despite its special aroma, vetiver oil also possesses various biological activities, such as antioxidant [ 4], antibacterial [ 5], and anti-inflammatory properties [ 6], making it beneficial in aromatherapy [ 7].
5: In fact, the annual market demand of vetiver oil was estimated at up to 250 tons, worth approximately $200 million per year [ 8], while the worldwide herbal cultivating area has reached nearly 10,000 hectares [ 9].
6: Because of these introductory facts, many researchers have focused on the innovation of extraction techniques to reduces production costs as well as environmental pollution.
