0: The trunk of  L. formosana can secrete an oleoresin due to insects, artificial tapping, or other mechanical damage.
1: L. formosana oleoresin is made up of a solid resin component and a volatile component.
2: The essential oil from the oleoresin can be obtained by hydrodistillation.
3: L. formosana resin has numerous medical applications in Asian folk medicine, such as being a promoter of blood circulation, an alleviator of blood stasis, an analgesic, and an anti-inflammatory and wound-healing agent [ 3, 4].
4: L. formosana oleoresin has been the source of several abietane diterpenoids [ 13], oleanane and lupane triterpenoids with cytotoxic [ 14], antifungal [ 15], glycogen phosphorylase inhibitory [ 16], and nuclear factor of activated T cell (NFAT) transcription factor inhibitory [ 17] activities.

7: The main components of essential oil are the monoterpenes -pinene (22.434.1%), -pinene (15.723.8%), myrcene (up to 10.9%), and limonene (3.48.4%); and the sesquiterpene ( E)-caryophyllene (7.826.5%) [ 15, 19].
