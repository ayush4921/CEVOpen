0: Lantana camara Lin ( L.  camara, Verbenaceae) is an invasive weed present in many countries [ 1, 2, 3].
1: This thorny shrub grows from 2 to 5 m high; its ripe fruits are blackish and its flowers are found in a variety of colors, with its flowering and fruiting seasons occurring almost year-round [ 4].
2: L. camara is commonly used as an ornamental plant [ 5, 6], but also in traditional medicine in many countries [ 7].
3: Indeed, its leaves are used in infusions or decoctions to treat sores, measles, intestinal worms, fever [ 8], ulcer, malaria, and rheumatism [ 5, 9].
4: The powdered root is used for stomach ache [ 8], while lotion made from the roots is used in the treatment of toothache and ulcers [ 10].
5: Whole plant infusions are also used to treat bronchitis [ 8].
6: Different solvent extracts produced from  L. camara have already been studied, showing the occurrence of flavonoids, triterpenes, and glycosides [ 5].
7: These extracts and compounds were described as having interesting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory [ 11, 12, 13], antibacterial, cytotoxic, antifungal, anthelmintic [ 14, 15, 16, 17, 18], and insecticidal properties [ 19, 20].
8: Some studies have also focused on  L. camara essential oils, showing the high diversity in essential oil compositions depending on plant growth area.
9: The major compounds reported were sesquiterpenes, such as ( E)--caryophyllene, -humulene, davanone, and germacrene D [ 15, 21, 22, 23].
10: Some monoterpenes were also found in high proportions, such as thymol, sabinene, and limonene [ 24, 25, 26].
