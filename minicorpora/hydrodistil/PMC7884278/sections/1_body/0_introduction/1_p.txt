0: Essential oils (EOs) are a concentrated mixture of hydrophobic compounds in the oil phase, characterized by a strong odor ( 1).
1: They are secreted as secondary metabolites from different parts of aromatic plants, such as flowers, fruits, seeds, stems, and roots ( 2).
2: Hydrodistillation using the Clevenger type apparatus is the most common approach for the extraction of EOs ( 3).
3: Recently, a growing number of studies on different medical properties of EOs have been being performed ( 4).
4: For example, as flavorings in the food ( 5), larvicidal activity ( 6), anticancer drug discovery ( 7), antioxidant properties ( 8), and antifungal bioassays ( 9).
5: In addition to such uses, EOs possess antibacterial effects against human pathogens such as  Staphylococcus aureus, Escherichia coli, Pseudomonas aeruginosa and  Klebsiella pneumoniae ( 10,  11).
