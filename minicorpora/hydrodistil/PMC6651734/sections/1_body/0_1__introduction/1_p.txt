0: The genus  Zanthoxylum, family of Rutaceae, contains approximately 250 species that are distributed in subtropical and tropical climates in America, Africa, Asia, and Australia [ 1].
1: This genus is well-known for its interesting biological properties, including antioxidant, antimicrobial, antifungal, and anticancer properties [ 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7].
2: These properties help to explain the extensive use of these plants in traditional medicine to treat a range of diseases, including anemia, cancer, sickle cell disease, infertility, rheumatism, osteoarthritis, and dysentery [ 8].
3: Furthermore, the fruits that species of the genus  Zanthoxylum produce are consumed as food condiments in China [ 9, 10].
