0: Lavender ( Lavandula angustifolia Mill.)
1: is a perennial herb and a member of the Lamiaceae (Labiatae) family that is native to the Mediterranean region and has been grown all over the world because of the huge market for essential oils.
2: Lavender essential oil is widely used in fragrances and commodities including colognes, skin lotions, soaps, food flavorings, perfumes and aromatherapeutic medicines [ 1].
3: Lavender is also widely cultivated in China, and its main producing area is in Yili, Xinjiang.
4: The  Lavandula genus is divided into 37 varieties according to the shape of the leaves, corolla morphology, calyces and bract in Lavender: The genus Lavandula [ 2].
5: Only the essential oils of three lavender species ( Lavandula angustifolia,  Lavandula latifolia and  Lavandula hybrid) play an important role in the perfume and cosmetics industry [ 3].
6: The essential oils of lavender are mainly produced from glands on the surface of the flowers and the leaves [ 2].
