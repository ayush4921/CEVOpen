0: Peppermint ( M. piperita) and chamomile ( C. recutita) plants were grown in fields of the Institute for Medicinal Plant Research Dr. Josif Pancic, Pancevo, South Banat, Serbia (445228.4 N 204156.2 E and 445221.9 N 204150.1 E, respectively).
1: Peppermint stolons were hand transplanted in the autumn of 2016 in rows, and at 10 cm depth, and 70 cm spacing.
2: The stolons originated from a large-scale peppermint production unit at the Institute.
3: Chamomile was sown at the beginning of October 2016 and at a sowing rate of 23 kg/ha.
4: Winter wheat ( Triticum vulgare Vill.)
5: was the pre-crop of both plants and fertilization was applied immediately after deep autumn plowing (November 2016) using compost (25 t/ha).
6: The soil type was chernozem with 2.3% humus, while total nitrogen content was 0.19%.
7: The pH was 6.4 in H 2O, and 5.4 in KCl.
8: Ammonium lactate-extractable (available) P 2O 5 and K 2O contents were 36 mg kg 1 and 362 mg kg 1, respectively.
9: Irrigation was also applied three times during the growing season and in the amount of 20 L/ha.
