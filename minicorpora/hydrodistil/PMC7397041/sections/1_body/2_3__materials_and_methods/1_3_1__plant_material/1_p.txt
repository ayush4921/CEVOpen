0: The  E. pavieana was cultivated in a commercial local farm in the Trad province, Thailand.
1: The plant was collected and identified by an expert herbalist.
2: The whole plant of  E. pavieana was harvested in August 2015.
3: The fresh rhizomes of  E. pavieana were washed to remove soil and chopped into small pieces.
4: The chopped rhizomes (100 kg) were fed into a vertical hydrodistillation machine (HVAC Engineering Co. Ltd., Pathumthani, Thailand) for 10 h. The essential oil was stored at 30 C in an airtight container for further use.
5: The total amount of essential oil was recorded.
6: The yield percentage was calculated on a fresh weight basis.
