0: Monarda fistulosa was collected in the month of August 2019 during the flowering and fruiting stages on the south side of Baldy Mountain, Gallatin Valley, MT, USA (45.7674 N, 110.9438 W) at an elevation of ~1750 m above sea level.
1: Flowers and leaves were air-dried for 710 days at room temperature away from direct sunlight before hydrodistillation.
2: Botanical identification of the plant material was performed by botanist Robyn Klein (Montana State University, Bozeman, MT, USA), who has extensive experience in the taxonomical identification of  Lamiaceae species.
