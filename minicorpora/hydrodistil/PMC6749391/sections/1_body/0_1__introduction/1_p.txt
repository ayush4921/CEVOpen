0: Pinus eldarica, also known as the Tehran pine, is a coniferous tree of the family Pinaceae.
1: The trees grow to a height of 1215 m and have a brownish-gray or light gray bark.
2: The needles are paired, medium green, and reach a length of 69 cm.
3: The cones may be either solitary or in pairs.
4: P. eldarica is native to west Asia and is adapted to warm and dry climates.
5: The trees grow on a wide variety of soil types and have been extensively planted in Iran [ 1, 2].
6: Pines have been used in medicine and industry throughout human life.
7: Pine needle essential oils (EOs) are used to add odors to products in the soap and perfume manufacturing industry.
8: Previously, the analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic effects of pine EOs have been reported [ 3].
