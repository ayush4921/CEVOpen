0: The Zingiberaceae family comprises approximately 52 genera and 1600 species and is mainly distributed in the tropical and subtropical regions [ 6, 7].
1: Many species of the Zingiberaceae are rich in essential oil and cultivated for their various applications in dyes, spices, ornamental, cosmetics, medicine, and food industries [ 7, 8].
2: The essential oils from the Zingiberaceae plants have been demonstrated to have multiple bioactivities, such as antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, anticancer, antimutagenic, analgesic, anti-allergic, anti-ulcer, insecticidal, and immunomodulatory activities [ 5, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12].
