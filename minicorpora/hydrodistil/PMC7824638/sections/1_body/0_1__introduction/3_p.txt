0: Aniba rosaeodora Ducke, Lauraceae is a large tree that reaches 30 m in height, with yellow-brown bark (hence the name rosewood) and grows in the Amazon region ( Figure 1A) [ 5].
1: Rosewood essential oil ( Figure 1B) is widely used in the perfumery industry and its extraction for industrial purposes began in the interior of the state of Para, Brazil, around 1930 [ 6].
2: All parts of the tree are fragrant, although only the trunk wood is harvested and hydrodistilled to obtain rosewood oil, a valuable product.
3: The species has been used in the Amazon as a medicinal plant to control epileptic seizures, to compose regional fragrances and as an ornamental plant [ 7].
