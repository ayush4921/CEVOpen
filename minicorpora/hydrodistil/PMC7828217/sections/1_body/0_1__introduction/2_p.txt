0: Frankincense, the oleogum resin of  Boswellia spp., is a multicomponent mixture containing 515% essential oil, 2530% ether-insoluble compounds such as polysaccharides, and 5566% ether-soluble pure resin, containing therapeutically interesting triterpenoids [ 1, 4].
1: Of particular interest are boswellic acids, which belong to the class of pentacyclic triterpenic acids and are exclusively occurring in the genus  Boswellia with contents up to 25% [ 5].
2: Boswellic acids are also highly abundant in cambium and epidermis, where they are assumed to be synthesized from amyrins [ 6].
