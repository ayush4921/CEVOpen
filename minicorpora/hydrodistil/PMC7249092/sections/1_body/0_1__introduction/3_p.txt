0: Since antiquity, the essential oils from  C. nepeta subsp.
1: nepeta leaves have been used as a fragrance and insect repellent [ 29].
2: The essential oils from the subspecies collected in Portugal, Serbia and Italy have been characterized for antifungal, antiproliferative, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects [ 30, 31, 32, 33].
3: In Tunisia only two subspecies, namely  C. nepeta subsp.
4: nepeta and  C. n. subsp.
5: glandulosum have been reported up to now; they have a very attractive smell and are often visited by insect pollinators.
6: As far as we know, nothing is reported on the chemical profile and biological efficacy of their essential oils.
