0: Previous studies on the secondary metabolites of  M. melissophyllum have focused on the essential oils obtained from the wild-growing populations originating from Italy, Slovakia, and Spain [ 1, 6, 7, 8, 9].
1: The literature concerning phenolic compounds in  M. melissophyllum is much scarce.
2: So far, coumarin and some phenolic acids have been identified in the herb, namely  o-coumaric, protocatechuic, chlorogenic, vanillic, caffeic, syringic, ferulic, sinapic, and cinnamic acids [ 10, 11, 12].
3: Among the flavonoids present in this plant, apigenin, kaempferol, luteolin, quercetin, and myricetin have been reported [ 13].
