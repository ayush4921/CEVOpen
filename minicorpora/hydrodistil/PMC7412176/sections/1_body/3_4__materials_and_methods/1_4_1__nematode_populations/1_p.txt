0: The populations of  Meloidogyne incognita and  M. javanica were collected from infested tomato greenhouses in Heraklion, Crete, and subsequently reared on tomato seedlings ( Solanum lycopersicum L.) cv.
1: Belladona in a glasshouse in Agricultural University of Athens, Greece.
2: All the seedlings were maintained in plastic pots in controlled conditions (25  2 C, 16 h light and 8 h dark).
3: The infestation of the seedlings took place when the plants were 6 weeks old, at the four-leaf stage.
4: After 40 days, roots were collected from plants and washed free of soil.
5: Eggs of  Meloidogyne populations were extracted using 1% sodium hypochlorite solution [ 29].
6: Second stage juveniles (J2s) were hatched in a modified Baremann funnel, where eggs were placed.
7: The J2s used in all tests were less than 2 days old.
