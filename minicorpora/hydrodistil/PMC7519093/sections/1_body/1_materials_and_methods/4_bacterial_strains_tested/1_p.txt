0: Twelve microorganisms, provided by the Iranian Research Organization for Science and Technology (IROST), were used to evaluate the antimicrobial activity of the essential oils: three Gram-positive bacteria,  Staphylococcus epidermidis (ATCC 12228),  Staphylococcus aureus (ATCC 29737) and  Bacillus subtilis (ATCC 6633), and six Gram-negative bacteria,  Klebsiella pneumoniae (ATCC 10031),  Shigella dysenteriae (PTCC 1188),  Pseudomonas aeruginosa (ATCC 27853),  Salmonella paratyphi-A serotype (ATCC 5702),  Proteus vulgaris (PTCC 1182) and  Escherichia coli (ATCC 10536).
1: Fungal strains were used as well:  Aspergillus niger (ATCC 16404),  Aspergillus brasiliensis (PTCC 5011) and  Candida albicans (ATCC 10231).
2: Bacterial strains were cultured overnight at 37 C in nutrient agar and fungi were cultured overnight at 30 C in Sabouraud dextrose agar.
