0: Among these, essential oils (EOs), defined as volatile secondary metabolites of plants, have been extensively used in traditional medicine due to of their many different biological properties, including antimicrobial and immunomodulatory ones.
1: EOs are produced by more than 17,500 species of plants from many angiosperm families, including  Asteraceae,  Lamiaceae,  Myrtaceae,  Rutaceae, and  Zingiberaceae, but only a small fraction of them are commercially available [ 3].
2: EOs are a complex mixture of terpenes, terpenoids, and phenylpropanoids, but they may also contain sulfur derivatives, fatty acids, and oxides [ 4].
