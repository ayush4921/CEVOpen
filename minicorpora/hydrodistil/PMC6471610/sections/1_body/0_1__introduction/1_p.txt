0: Since ancient times, nutmeg ( Myristica fragrans, f. Myristicaceae) has been used as a spice.
1: Its aphrodisiac effect is often mentioned [ 1].
2: In traditional medicine, nutmeg is used to treat rheumatism, pain, nausea, stomach cramps, and other illnesses [ 2].
3: A lot of research is being done in order to test the pharmacological effects of nutmeg.
4: Scientific sources say that nutmeg has antibacterial, insecticidal, antioxidant, anticancer, antidepressant, hepatoprotective, and many other effects [ 3, 4, 5, 6].
5: The most common studies about essential oils are of nutmeg, which is rich in terpenes, phenols, various organic acids, and other compounds.
6: Using different solvents, methods, and conditions, the composition of extracts and essential oils variations were studied [ 3, 7, 8, 9].
7: Nutmeg essential oil is colorless to pale-yellow with a specific odor.
8: The essential oil yield may be more than 10% [ 10] and varies by 515% [ 11].
9: Myristica fragrans essential oil has predominantly monoterpene hydrocarbons (-thujene, -pinene, sabinene), sesquiterpene hydrocarbons ( d-germacrene, trans--bergamotene), monoterpene alcohols (linalool, -terpineol,  cis-p-menth-2-en-1-ol, terpinen-4-ol), esters (-terpinyl acetate,  cis-sabinene hydrate acetate, citronellyl acetate), aromatics (eugenol, myristicin, elemicin, methoxyeugenol), and other compounds [ 5, 7, 8, 9, 12].
