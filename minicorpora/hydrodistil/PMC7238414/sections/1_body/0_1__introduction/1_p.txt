0: The genus  Eryngium L., which belongs to the subfamily Saniculoideae of the Apiaceae family, is represented by 230250 species widespread in Central Asia, America, and Central and Southeast Europe [ 1].
1: Among them, there are some species such as  E. campestre,  E. maritimum,  E. foetidum, and  E. creticum that have been used in the traditional medicines worldwide [ 2].
2: The pharmacological activities of  Eryngium species depend mainly on the presence of triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, phenolic acids, coumarin derivatives, acetylenes, and the essential oils [ 3, 4].
