0: Mango is economically important, being the third-largest agricultural product in India.
1: The cultivation of mango in India covers around 2.26 million acres, which is 40% of the total area used for Indian fruit production ( Figure 1b).
2: Over 30 varieties of mangoes are commercially available in India.
3: The huge diversity in Indian mango cultivation is due to the large number of cultivars (the country is home to approximately 1000 varieties, only a few of which are commercially cultivated) and wild varieties.
4: Apart from  Mangifera indica, many other species such as  M. khasiana,  M. andamanica,  M. camptosperma and  M. sylvatica have been reported in India [ 4].
