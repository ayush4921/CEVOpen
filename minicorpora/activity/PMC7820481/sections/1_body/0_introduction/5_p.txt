0: Thyme ( Thymus vulgaris) is a member of the family Lamiaceae.
1: Its oil mainly contains thymol, borneol, and carvacrol ( Jakiemiu et al., 2010).
2: It has antioxidative, antibacterial, and antimycotic properties ( Imelouane et al., 2009).
3: Solgi et al.
4: (2009) reported that thyme oils are used in the vase solution for prolonging the shelf life of gerbera ( Gerbera jasmeson cv.Dune) cut flowers.
5: Clove ( Syzygium aromaticum) belongs to the family Myrtaceae and has many medicinal properties such as antimicrobial and antiviral activities ( Osman et al.
6: (2020);  Devkota and Adhikor-Devkota, 2020;  Osman et al.
7: (2020);  Cui et al., 2018).
8: Clove plant is the main source of phenolic molecule as hydroxybenzoic acids, flavonoids, hydroxyphenylpropenes, and hydroxycinnamic acids ( Batiha et al., 2020).
9: Also, The EOs collected from flowers, buds, stems, and the leaves of the plant, showed different chemical composition, colour, and flavour ( Shahbazi and Shavisi, 2015).
10: The essential oils of clove have been shown antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant analgesic, antiparasitic, anti-convulsant, and anticarcinogenic properties ( Ramadan et al., 2013;  Sanuja et al., 2014;  Lekjing, 2016).
