0: Manuka ( Leptospermum scoparium), also known as kahikatoa, red manuka and tea tree, belongs to the  Myrtaceae plant family and is found throughout New Zealand and Australia [ 1].
1: Commonly grouped under tea trees, which also includes  Camellia sinensis, Kunzea ericoides,  Leptospermum petersonii and  Melaleuca alternifolia, these species have been used by the Maori, the Aboriginals and early European settlers as topical preparations for wounds, cuts, sores and skin diseases and as inhalations for colds and fevers.
2: Interest in the medicinal properties of manuka, particularly manuka honey, has grown over the last 30 years.
3: There is also a growing interest in the volatile oil due to its potential application as a medicinal agent or cosmetic ingredient [ 2, 3].
4: Currently, there has not been a critical review on the efficacy and potential applications of manuka oil while there have been extensive reviews on Manuka honey and its medical applications.
5: We aim to bridge this gap through this review, providing an up-to-date summary of recent developments in our understanding of the medicinal activities of manuka oil as well as its clinical efficacyand toxicity profile.
