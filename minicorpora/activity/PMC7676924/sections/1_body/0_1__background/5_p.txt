0: Essential oils are known for their antibacterial, antifungal, antiviral, insecticidal, and antioxidant properties [ 14].
1: They are widely used in traditional medicine and the food industry.
2: The main constituents of essential oils are terpenes, terpenoids, aromatic and aliphatic constituents, and some hydrocarbons, which also exhibit antimicrobial effects [ 15].
3: Studies have shown that essential oils target the cell wall membrane, thereby disturbing ATP production and pH homeostasis [ 16].
4: A study conducted by Ameya et al.
5: on the crude methanol and ethanol extracts of  E. kebericho demonstrated activity against clinical isolates of  Candida albicans and  Aspergillus flavus [ 11].
6: Though the research was done on the crude extract against the two strains, the effect on the other fungal strains is unknown, and there has been no study on the essential oil of E.  kebericho.
7: The aim of this study, therefore, was to evaluate the antifungal activity of the essential oil of  E. kebericho M. against  Candida albicans, Candida glabrata, Cryptococcus neoformans, and  Aspergillus flavus.
