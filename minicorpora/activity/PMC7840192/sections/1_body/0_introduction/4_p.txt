0: Lavandula angustifolia (Common Lavender, English Lavender, French lavender, Garden Lavender, Lavender) is a flowering plant in the  Lamiaceae family.
1: Medicinal parts are usually roots, leaves or fruit of the plants, as well as essential oil from fresh flowers and/or the inflorescence; the flowers collected just before opening and dried, the fresh flowers and the dried flowers.
2: Lavender oil has been reported to contain more than 100 components.
3: The essential oil (1%3%) of  Lavandula is rich in linalool and linalyl acetate.
4: Linalyl acetate is the major compound found in flowers.
5: The plant contains also rosmarinic acid and coumarin (LisBalchin,  2002).
6: Carvacrol (26.2%), limonene (19.6%), 1,8cineole (11.8%), terpinen4ol (7.6%), spathulenol (4.9%), pinene (4.2%), pcymene (4.2%), caryophyllene oxide (2.7%) and terpinolene (2.6%) were detected in  Lavandula angustifolia essential oil by Bakhsha, Mazandarani, Aryaei, Jafari, & Bayate,  2014.
