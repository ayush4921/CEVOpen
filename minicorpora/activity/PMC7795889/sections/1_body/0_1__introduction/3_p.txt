0: Rhynchanthus J. D. Hooker, a small genus in the family Zingiberaceae, has approximately four species and is mainly distributed in Myanmar, Indonesia, and Southern China [ 13, 14].
1: Rhynchanthus beesianus W. W. Smith, a perennial herb with tuberous rhizomes is cultivated as an edible, medicinal, and ornamental plant in Myanmar and Southern China [ 14, 15].
2: The rhizomes and tender leaves of  R. beesianus are used as edible spices and vegetables in Yunnan Province, China.
3: Its flower shape is peculiar, like a colorful and gorgeous brush, which can be used as fresh cut flowers or used for hanging cultivation.
4: The rhizome has been used in Chinese folk medicine and as an aromatic stomachic, commonly known as  diangaoliangjiang, to treat stomachache, backache, and indigestion [ 16, 17, 18].
5: Additionally, the EO from its rhizome has been used as an aromatic stomachic in China [ 17].
6: However, there is no study on the chemical composition and pharmacological activity of  R. beesianus, which could impede its exploitation in the industry.
7: Therefore, the purpose of this research is to study the chemical composition of the essential oil from  R. beesianus rhizome and evaluate its antibacterial, anti-inflammatory and enzyme inhibitory activities.
