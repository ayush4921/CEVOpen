0: Moringa oleifera is a genus of the fast-growing tropical deciduous plant of the Moringaceae family, with thick, tuberous roots, light green leaves and abundant flowering with elongated, pendulous fruits and seeds [ 1].
1: It is a native crop of northern India, although it is found in southwest Asia, southwest and northwest Africa and Madagascar.
2: It has long been a part of traditional horticulture, used mainly for ornamental purposes in cities along the Pacific coast of Mexico [ 2], as well as plantations in Bolivia, Argentina and elsewhere in the world [ 3].
3: It has 13 known species, with  Moringa oleifera, native to India, being one of the most studied and used for its nutritional, phytochemical and pharmacological properties.
4: According to ayurvedic medicine (traditional and alternative medicine of India) [ 4], it is attributed properties for the treatment of some diseases, such as asthma, epilepsy, eye and skin diseases, fever and haemorrhoids [ 5, 6, 7].
5: In fact, it is a medicinal plant traditionally known in the approach to malnutrition and other diseases [ 8].
