0: Psidium (Myrtaceae) comprises approximately 266 species, distributed in tropical and subtropical regions of the world.
1: Psidium taxa have great ecological, economic, and medicinal relevance due to their essential oils chemical diversity and biological potential.
2: This review reports 18   Psidium species growing around the world and the chemical and biological properties of their essential oils.
3: Chemically, 110 oil records are reported with significant variability of volatile constituents, according to their seasonality and collection sites.
4: Monoterpenes and sesquiterpenes with acyclic (C  10 and C  15),   p-menthane, pinane, bisabolane, germacrane, caryophyllane, cadinane, and aromadendrane skeleton-types, were the primary constituents.
5: The essential oils showed various biological activities, including antioxidant, antifungal, antibacterial, phytotoxic, larvicidal, anti-inflammatory, and cytotoxic properties.
6: This review contributes to the   Psidium species rational and economic exploration as natural sources to produce new drugs.
