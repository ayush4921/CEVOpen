0: In recent years, reviews have been published referring to essential oils from Myrtaceaes largest genera, such as  Myrcia, Eugenia, and  Syzygium [ 15, 16].
1: A review of essential oils from Myrtaceae describing various chemical analyses of  Psidium species has also been published [ 17].
2: However, there is no review focusing jointly on the chemical compositions and biological activities of essential oils of  Psidium species.
3: Due to the chemical and biological significance of  Psidium species, in this review, there are 110 chemical analyses reported of 18  Psidium species growing widespread in the world and their biological activities.
