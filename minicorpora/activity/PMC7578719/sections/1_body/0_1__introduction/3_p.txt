0: In Africa, goat weed has been used traditionally to relieve constipation and fever and applied as a wound dressing agent as well as an antiulcer agent.
1: In Togo, this herb is used to treat measles and snake bite, whereas in Nigeria, it is used for skin diseases, wound healing, diarrhoea, pain around the navel in children, and even used to treat HIV/AIDS [ 4].
2: The leaves are consumed as vegetables and also to prevent tetanus [ 5].
3: It is also used in the medication of pneumonia, antitoxin of snake venom, typhoid fever, malaria fever, sore throat, and candidiasis.
4: The roots are used in the treatment of tumours, lithiasis, and diarrhoea in a baby.
5: Moreover, the flower is used to relieve itching, insomnia, cough, vermifuge, tonics, and antibug parasite [ 6].
6: Besides its benefits in medication, this weed is also utilized in agriculture as an organic material which can increase a soil nutrient composition.
7: A study evaluating this weed administration in the bokashi preparation toward the cultivation and the nutrient composition of a tomato plant demonstrates a significant effect in a mass of the tomato fruits [ 7].
