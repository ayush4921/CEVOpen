1: The  Canarium schweinfurthii or aiele tree grows in the equatorial forest region of Cameroon, Central African Republic, Gabon, and Congo [ 8].
2: All these species produce a gum that solidifies into resin [ 9].
3: The resin is distilled to produce essential oil commonly used in traditional medicine and perfumery because of its lavender smell.
4: Okoume resin is used to purify water, to ripen abscesses, and is applied as a substitute for incense.
5: The bark can be employed as an antiseptic, astringent, or antidiarrheal agent [ 10, 11].
6: In addition, aiele resin is used in traditional medicine for the treatment of various diseases such as cutaneous lesions and microbial infestations.
7: It is also used for its emollient, stimulating, and diuretic properties [ 12].
