0: Berry fruits are rich sources of phenolic compounds.
1: Among berry fruit plants,  Elaeagnus umbellata Thunb.
2: is a member of the  Elaeagnaceae family with a high medicinal value that is native to Southern Europe and Central Asia [ 10].
3: It is abundantly found in Himalayan regions of Pakistan as well [ 11].
4: The  Elaeagnus species are traditionally used as antioxidant, anticancer, antinociceptive, anti-inflammatory, anti-mutagenic, anti-ulcerogenic, antimicrobial, antidiabetic, and neuroprotective agents [ 12 14].
5: Previously we have evaluated the antioxidant, antidiabetic, and anticholinesterase potential of different extracts and isolated compounds (rutin, epigallocatechin gallate, epigallocatechin, quercetin, morin, ellagic acid, catechin, chlorogenic acid, and pyrogallol) of  E. umbellata [ 13,  14].
6: The floral volatiles and biological activities of  E. umbellata and  E. angustifolia L. have been reported [ 15 18].
7: A review of the literature revealed that no previous studies have been performed on the essential oil of fruit of  E. umbellata.
