0: The  Virola genus of the Myristicaceae family is found throughout tropical America, where these species have diverse local ethnobotanical uses [ 3].
1: Seed oils of  Virola are used as machinery lubricants or soap ingredients or to make candles with intense light, little smoke, and fragrant smells.
2: The seeds and arils can also be used to flavor beverages, such as chocolate drinks, while  Virola wood is used for construction and carpentry [ 3].
3: Besides their non-medicinal benefits,  Virola are perhaps best known for their hallucinogenic properties, which are often used in South American indigenous cultural practices [ 4].
4: Moreover, the reddish exudate of various  Virola spp.
5: is applied to treat skin conditions, alleviate tooth pain, and soothe colic, and as an anti-bleeding substance to treat ulcerating sores and wounds, as well as hemorrhoids.
6: Additionally, the seed oils have many folk medicine applications, such as the treatment of asthma, rheumatism, tumors of the joints, intestinal worms, skin diseases, erysipelas, hemorrhoids, and bad breath [ 3].
7: Therefore, in the last few years, growing research has evaluated the biological effects of species from the  Virola genus, identifying new promising bioactive compounds.
