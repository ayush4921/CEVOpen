0: Background Inflammation is a host defense mechanism in the body after it is infected and damaged.
1: If inflammation is not treated in time, then it may cause a variety of diseases, such as cancer and autoimmune diseases.
2: Herbal essential oils are natural extracts that can suppress inflammation effectively and are expected to be used in therapeutic drugs for anti-inflammatory diseases in the future.
3: Aim of the review.
4: We review the anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects of essential oils derived from 16 herbs.
5: Materials and methods.
6: We searched the literature of the fields of anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory herbal essential oil activity published in English within the past five years via databases (PubMed, EMBASE, Scopus, and The Web of Science).
7: Results A total of 1932 papers were found by searching, and 132 papers were screened after removing duplicates and reading article titles.
8: Fifteen articles met the requirements to be included in this review.
9: Among those selected, 11 articles reported in vivo research results, and 10 articles showed research results.
10: Conclusion Essential oils extracted from herbs can reduce inflammation by regulating the release of inflammatory cytokines involved in multiple signalling pathways.
11: Herbal essential oils are expected to be developed as anti-inflammatory drugs.
