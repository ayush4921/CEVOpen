0: The use of fragrance is ubiquitous and is a global human phenomenon.
1: Over the course of time, countless numbers of flavors and fragrances have found their way into everyday life, notably into foods, beverages and confectionery items; into personal care products (soaps, toothpastes, mouthwashes, deodorants, bath lotions and shampoos), perfumes, and other cosmetics as well as pharmaceutical formulations.
2: Indeed, flavors and aromas are added to make such products more attractive or to mask the taste or smell of less pleasant ones [ 1].
