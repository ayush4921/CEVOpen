0: South Sudan is one of the famous crude oil producing countries falling 3 rd after Nigeria and Angola in Africa [ 6] and 83 rd among the 171 oil producing countries worldwide [ 7].
1: Oil is the lifeline of its economy for now and over the medium term [ 6].
2: For example, between 2008 and 2011, oil exports accounted for 98% of government revenue [ 6].
3: This position has fundamentally changed her economy from agriculture to industrialization.
4: South Sudan has a production capacity of 298,000/390,000 barrels per day [ 8,  9] including Sudd wetland.
5: Sudd is the largest wetland in the whole world and covers 57,000 Km 2 that makes up approximately 5% of the total land area of the Republic of South Sudan (648,000 Km 2) [ 10,  11].
6: The area of the wetland is larger than countries such as Switzerland, Belgium, the Netherlands, and Singapore [ 12].
7: Owing to this enormous size, the Sudd ecosystems are of vast socioeconomic, cultural, and biological importance locally, nationally, and internationally.
8: This accounts for why it was designated as a Ramsar site in 2006 [ 10] making it an essential nature conservation area.
