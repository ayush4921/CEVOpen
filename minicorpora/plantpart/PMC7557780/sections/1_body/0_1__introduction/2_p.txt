0: Bacterial wilt, a xylem disease causing vascular dysfunction, is caused by Rs, a Gram-negative plant pathogenic bacterium infecting a large range of economically important hosts.
1: The symptomatology in tomato includes wilting of upper leaves, development of adventitious roots along the stem, and wilting of the whole plant in hot humid environmental conditions.
2: A longitudinal section of the stem reveals the presence of a brown discoloration of vascular tissue with leaking of drops of bacterial exudate.
3: Rs can survive for a long time in the soil and infect the plant through wounds; dissemination can also occur through infected propagation material and water.
4: On the basis of scientific and economic impact, Rs is ranked second in the list of top ten phytobacteria [ 11] and was classified as a bioterrorism agent in the USA in 2002 [ 12].
5: In tomato, its ability to spread through the seed is controversial [ 13, 14], although, recently, Dey et al.
6: [ 15] highlighted the transmissibility of Rs in tomato and brinjal seeds, confirming previous evidence [ 16, 17] and thus assuming its economic importance for seed trade and exchange.
