0: The miracle plant was first discovered in West and Central Africa, specifically in countries like Congo, Ghana and Nigeria ( He et al., 2015;  Sulaini and Sabran, 2018).
1: The plant is known as  Agbayun and  Uni respectively among the Yoruba and Igbo people of southern Nigeria ( Obafemi et al., 2019).
2: Miracle plant grows to a height of about 3 m in cultivation, and 6.1 m in a native habitat.
3: Leaves are 510 cm long and 23.7 cm wide.
4: Flowers are cream-colored but turn dark-red or brown with maturity.
5: The flowers are bisexual.
6: The calyx is made up of four to five sepals, the corolla four to five petals, and the androecium five stamens.
7: The gynoecium stands erect with an unspectacular stigma ( Xingwei et al., 2016).
8: The ripe fruit is red in color.
9: The miracle berry is not as common as other berry fruits such as blueberry, blackberry, cranberry and grapes .
10: The berry is about the size of a coffee bean, roughly 2 cm long and 1 cm wide.
11: It has relatively large seed which is encapsulated by a translucent pulp that is covered by a thin skin.
12: The sweetening effect is contained in the pulp.
13: The fruits are usually clustered at the ends of the branches ( Figure 1) ( He et al., 2016).
