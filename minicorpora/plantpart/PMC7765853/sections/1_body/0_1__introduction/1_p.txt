0: Origanum vulgare L., commonly oregano, is one of the most renowned aromatic species, with a strong traditional background as a spice and medicinal plant, but also as a well-established source of valuable plant-based drugs in modern phytotherapy.
1: This  Lamiaceae plant is native to Europe, the North of the African continent, and most of temperate Asia, but the hotspot of its diversity is situated in the Mediterranean region and predominantly in Turkey [ 1].
2: It has also been introduced in North America, where it grows in several states along the East and West coast [ 2].
3: Commercial  O. vulgare is either wild-collected or cultivated, with Turkey being the most important supplier of Mediterranean oregano.
4: However, the common name of oregano is employed for several other species of the genus ( Origanum onites or Turkish oregano,  O. majorana or sweet marjoram,  O. minutiflorum or Spartan oregano,  O. syriacum var.
5: bevanii or Israeli oregano) or even belonging to other genus ( Thymus, Satureja, Lippia, Coridothymus) [ 3].
