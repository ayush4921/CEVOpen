0: The intensification of animal husbandry and the globalization of trade have contributed to the extensive and improper use of antibiotics to combat infectious diseases.
1: Since the end of the 1990s, awareness of this risk increased, prompting a gradual mobilization of the international community [ 1].
2: It has become an important issue as it has been recognized as a selective pressure, driving the accelerated emergence of bacterial resistance worldwide [ 2, 3].
3: Prudent use of antibiotics in the future is urgently needed.
4: It should be addressed in a One Health perspective, given that antibiotic resistance in humans, food, environment, and animals are connected, and exchange may continuously occur [ 4, 5].
