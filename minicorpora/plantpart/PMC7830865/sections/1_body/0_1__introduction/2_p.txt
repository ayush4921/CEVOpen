1: Black pepper is a perennial, woody vine that easily grows in shaded places, with indeterminate growth, but some studies report that some plantations limit their height up to 9 m. If the plant touches the ground, the root can come out of the leaf nodes [ 10].
2: Cultivars of black pepper present differences in leaf shape, stem, flowers, as well as the fruit size and thickness [ 11, 12, 13].
3: The species presents several cultivars identified in the literature, with dozens of these cultivars explored in the main production areas of Brazil, such as Apra, Bragantina, Cingapura, Clonada, Equador, Guajarina, Iacara, Kottanadan, Kuthiravally, and Uthirankota, among others [ 13, 14, 15, 16, 17].
