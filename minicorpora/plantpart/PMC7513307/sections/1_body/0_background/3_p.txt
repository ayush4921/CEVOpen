0: In this study, we have focused on an essential oil extracted from  Eryngium triquetrum.
1: This Apiaceae is an endemic species of northern Africa, distributed in all parts of Algeria; the green perennial plant becomes a bluish-purplish color when ripe.
2: This species grows particularly well in rocky pastures.
3: It was considered as a ruderal species, which the local people call choukzerk [ 20].
4: No traditional use for this thorny plant has been reported by the Algerian population.
5: However, species of the genus  Eryngium are used as a folk remedy for the treatment of various inflammatory disorders, and as an emetic infusion, antidote for poisons, hypoglycemic, antitussive and diuretic agents [ 21 24].
6: Species of  Eryngium have been subjects of many phytochemical studies.
7: For example, hexanic extract of  E. campestre showed anti-trypanosomal and leishmanial activities [ 23].
8: Eryngium caeruleum extract displayed antimicrobial activities [ 25].
9: The essential oil of  E. triquetrum for which the chemical composition was reported exhibits a moderate antibacterial action against Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria [ 23].
