0: Fennel ( Foeniculum vulgare) is an aromatic plant that belongs to the  Apiaceae family and is considered one of the oldest plants cultivated in the world.
1: This is an annual, biennial or perennial plant native to the Mediterranean area grown for its aromatic fruits, which are used as culinary spices.
2: It grows especially well in coastal climates and riverbanks [ 19, 20].
3: Fennel essential oils are used as a flavoring agent in foods such as beverages, bread, pickles, pastries and cheese.
4: It is also used as a component in cosmetics and pharmaceuticals.
5: Fennel medicines and fennel essential oils have hepatoprotective effects as well as antispasmodic effects.
6: Additionally, volatile fennel oil is known for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, analgesic and antioxidant activity [ 21].
7: The antioxidant and antimicrobial activity of volatile fennel oil is offered by the high content of trans-anethole (63.30%), pinene (11.11%) and fenchone (8.32%) [ 20, 21, 22, 23, 24, 25]
