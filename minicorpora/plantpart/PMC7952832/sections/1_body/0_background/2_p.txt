0: The chemical composition of seed extracts of  N. sativa seed varies from one country to another and also from one region in a country to another (Shariq et al.
1: 2015).
2: The major components in  N. sativa seed are fatty oils and aliphatic hydrocarbons, arachidonic acid, tocopherols, -linolenic acid, and essential oil, which contains carvacrol, -cymene, thymoquinone, -pinene, and indazole-type alkaloids, isoquinoline alkaloids, as well as dolabellane-type diterpene alkaloids.
3: The food and therapeutic applications of  N. sativa oil and seed have a long history in many cultures.
4: Islamic medicine also cataloged the numerous benefits of this plant (Sharma et al.
5: 2009).
6: Its compounds have been shown to have various pharmacological effects on different body parts.
7: Empirical studies have reported that the seed oil and seed extracts have antimicrobial activities, which some workers have attributed to the presence of compounds such as thymohydroquinone and melanin (Kalim 2013; Al Yahya 1986).
8: The seed extract contains numerous antioxidants like carvacrol, t-anethole, and 4-terpineol, and their activities have been reported in different studies (Awadalla  2012).
9: Also, available reports have shown the anti-inflammatory, anticancer, anti-diabetic, and hepatoprotective activities of its oil and extracts (Umar et al.
10: 2012; Peng et al.
11: 2013; Abdelmeguid et al.
12: 2010).
