0: A characteristic Myrtle liqueur is one of the main typical productions from Sardinia.
1: Some parts of the plant are used in the food industry for flavoring meat and sauces, and its leaves have been extensively used in perfume and cosmetic industries [ 3, 4].
2: Myrtle has also been intensively studied for its essential oil obtained from the whole aerial part of the plant [ 5], leaves [ 3, 6], flowers [ 3], and berries [ 4].
3: Different chemical profiles according to different plant parts sampled, or different test populations, have been registered.
4: Normally, the main compounds in leaf and flower essential oils are -pinene, linalool, and 1,8-cineole, while myrtenyl acetate is often the main compound in white berry essential oil [ 7, 8, 9, 10].
