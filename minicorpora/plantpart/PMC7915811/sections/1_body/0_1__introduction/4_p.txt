1: In the food industry there are many sweeteners (natural, semi-natural or chemical).
2: Each sweetener has its advantages and disadvantages, such as sucrose which is a natural sugar produced from sugar beets or sugar canes.
3: It is easily absorbed and provides energy [ 1, 4, 15, 16].
4: A huge amount of sucrose in jelly can crystallize and form a layer of sugar on top.
5: To avoid this, polyols are used (sorbitol, glycerol or others), they do not let the sugar crystals to form [ 1, 17].
6: Artificial sweeteners, although some are sweeter than sucrose, cannot deceive the brain and an organism understands what type of calories were gained and which hormones have to be released from the cells in order to break down the consumed sweeteners.
7: These artificial sweeteners can lead to seizures, headaches, and attention deficit disorders (aspartame as an example) [ 18].
