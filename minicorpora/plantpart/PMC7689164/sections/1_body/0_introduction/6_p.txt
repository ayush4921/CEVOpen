0: The ethnobotanical use of plants of the genus Mentha is common in Morocco.
1: The first family of aromatic plants used by the local population of Ouazzane is Lamiaceae, with 40% of species belonging to the genus Mentha [ 20].
2: Traditional medicine is the first recourse for 78% of the population in Errachidia province, as well as in other regions where medicinal plants are available at low-cost [ 21].
3: Women (75%) and prescribers are the main consumers of medicinal plants [ 21,  22].
4: The preparation of traditional drugs from medicinal plants is done using different methods such as the infusion, the decoction, and the external application, while oral administration is the most used route of administration [ 20].
5: Leaves are often the part of the plant used in pharmacopeia [ 11,  13,  23,  24].
6: The common use of Mentha leaves in pharmacopeia could be due the ease and quickness of leaves harvesting.
7: Moreover, leaves are the center of the photosynthetic phenomenon and secondary metabolites storage [ 20].
