0: Essential oils (EOs), biosynthesized by aromatic medicinal plants as secondary metabolites, are the oily liquids that contain a mixture of compounds [ 5].
1: They possess various bioactive properties such as antimicrobial, antiviral, antimycotic, antioxygenic, antiparasitic, antiviral, and insecticidal [ 6,  7].
2: Initially, they were used because of their fragrance and flavour, but nowadays they are gaining popularity and widely used for a variety of purposes including household cleaning products, personal beauty care, prolonging the shelf life of food, in aquaculture and as natural medicines.
