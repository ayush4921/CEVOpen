0: Coriander ( Coriandrum sativum L.) is an annual herb of the Apiaceae family that is used in many industries.
1: It is a culinary and medicinal plant, native to the Near East and Mediterranean area, but it has spread to India and China.
2: Nowadays, coriander is a common herb around the world [ 1].
3: Coriander has a number of therapeutic properties (e.g., antibacterial, anticancer, antidiabetic, antimutagenic, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, analgesic, anticonvulsant, blood pressure-lowering, cholesterol-lowering, and sedative activities [ 2, 3]) desired in the medical and pharmaceutical industries, due to the presence of specific metabolites.
4: Coriander has been used in traditional medicine for ages to counteract several disorders, such as dysentery, dyspepsia, giddiness spasm, neuralgia, and gastric complaints [ 4].
5: Moreover, coriander, overall, is used as a spice.
6: Due to its spicy citrus flavor [ 5], it is also used as a flavoring agent in food products and in the perfume and cosmetic industries.
7: All of the above applications make coriander an economically important plant [ 6].
