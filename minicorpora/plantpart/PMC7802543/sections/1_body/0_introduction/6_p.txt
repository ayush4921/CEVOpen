0: Ferulago angulata known as Chavir (Azarbani et al.,  2014) is a native plant in some parts of Iran (Sodeifian et al.,  2011).
1: The genus  Ferulago belongs to the Apiaceae family and is used in folk medicine for sedative, tonic, digestive, and antiparasitic effects (Taran et al.,  2010).
2: There have been some published reports on its significant antibacterial, antioxidant, and antidiabetic properties, and, traditionally, it was added to dairy and oil ghee to prevent decay as a strong preservative and to increase the shelf life of dairy products besides adding a pleasant taste to them (Alizadeh et al.,  2019; Azarbani et al.,  2014).
