The three largest metropolitan areas in South Africa present different challenges and opportunities for dragonfly conservation. ( a) A former sugarcane field now part of the Kenneth Stainbank Nature Reserve, ( b) a dragonfly conservation pond in Pietermaritzburg Botanical Gardens, ( c) a high elevation mountain stream within the Table Mountain National Park, ( d) a heavily impacted wetland in the lowlands of the Cape Floristic Region, ( e) an urban lake in the heart of the Tshwane Metropolitan Area, formerly used as an irrigation reservoir, and ( f) an impacted stream in a suburban area now being rehabilitated.
