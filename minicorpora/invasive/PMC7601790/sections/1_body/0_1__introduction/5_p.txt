0: The Mexican poppy,  Argemone ochroleuca Sweet (Papaveraceae), is a worldwide invasive annual herb native to Central America.
1: It grows in a wide range of soil types but normally dominates sandy soils, and can grow well in nutrient-poor soils.
2: It is naturalized in several semiarid regions.
3: The seeds are dispersed through wind, water, birds, and agricultural practices.
4: In Mexico,  A. ochroleuca is used in traditional medicine to treat several diseases [ 29].
5: Today, this weed invades many countries of Africa, Asia, Europe, Oceania, North America, and South America [ 30, 31, 32].
6: In the southwest of Saudi Arabia, the species has invaded a wide range of habitat types including agro-ecosystems, fallow lands, roadsides, wastelands, wetlands, gravelly and sandy plains [ 32, 33, 34].
7: The spread of  A. ochroleuca has been ascribed to disturbance, overgrazing, and invasive competency [ 32, 34].
8: The  Mexican poppy has been reported to possess allelopathic activity which enables it to invade and dominate new habitats [ 33, 35].
