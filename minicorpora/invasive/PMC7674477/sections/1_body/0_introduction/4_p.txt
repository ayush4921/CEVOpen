0: Senecio madagascariensis Poir.
1: (Asteraceae) is a species native to South Africa and Madagascar that is currently considered invasive in Australia 15, south-east Asia 16, South America 17,  18 and North America 19.
2: In South America, this species was recorded for the first time in Argentina, where it was found in the port area in Bahia Blanca (Buenos Aires province) in the 1940s 17.
3: In about 30 years,  S. madagascarienis successfully invaded the north of Buenos Aires province and nearby provinces.
4: Currently, this species is widely distributed in northern and central Argentina, and southeastern Brazil 20.
