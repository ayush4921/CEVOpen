0: Mammalian prey were identified to species, genus, family or order level, based on  cuticula, cell structure, cross section of guard hairs 47, and teeth, if present in the sample 48.
1: Birds were determined to taxonomic order level using feathers 49.
2: Amphibians, reptiles, insects, vegetation and larger undigested body parts of mammalian and bird prey were identified based on field guides and reference collections 48,  50,  51.
3: Fruits, berries and cereals were classified based on seed structures using reference collections from native plants and field books with illustrations for comparison.
4: Mammalian bones and tissue without guard hairs or other key-identification traits were assigned to Mammalian prey, with no further taxonomic classification.
5: We visually estimated the proportion of each diet category in individual samples, relative to the full volume and weight of each faecal sample.
6: Small stones and host hairs were not included in the diet analysis since these components were classified as Non-diet.
