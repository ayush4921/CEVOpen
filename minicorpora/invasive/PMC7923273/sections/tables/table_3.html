Estimated mean response and associated 95% confidence intervals of the soil characteristics analyzed for plots invaded and uninvaded by S. gigantea in two habitat types. Values were obtained from “least squares means” outputs of mixed models fitted to the variables. For both habitat types, riparian vegetation, and semi-natural grassland, ect. mean responses are shown for plots invaded and uninvaded by S. gigantea. Riparian vegetation habitats contained 24 plot-pairs in total, while semi-natural grasslands contained 28 plot-pairs in total. Values for OM content, total C (g/kg dry soil), total N (g/kg dry soil), Aphelenchidae, Aphelenchoididae, Diphtherophora, total fungi, Ascomycota, Basidiomycota, Chytridiomycota, and total bacteria were back transformed from logarithmic values to the original scale. Aphelenchidae, Aphelenchoididae, and Diphtherophora are expressed in numbers (#) per 100 g dry soil. For Diphtherophora, 8 plot-pairs from semi-natural grasslands were excluded from analysis. Fungal biomass is expressed as mg ergosterol per kg soil. Total fungal density, Ascomycota, Basidiomycota, Chytridiomycota, and total bacterial density are expressed in μg DNA per 100 g dry soil. Significant p-values (< 0.05) are indicated in bold.
