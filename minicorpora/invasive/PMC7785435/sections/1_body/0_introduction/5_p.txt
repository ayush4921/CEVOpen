0: The tree tobacco  Nicotiana glauca Graham (Solanaceae) is a cosmopolitan invasive fast-growing plant, and it is a native perennial shrub of northwest Argentina and Bolivia ( Issaly et al., 2020).
1: Now,  N. glauca is widely naturalized in warm temperate regions of the world ( Florentine et al., 2006) and has spread worldwide through human activity.
2: It is a successful invasive shrub in semi-arid areas.
3: It has the ability to grow in a broad range of open and disturbed habitats, including roadsides, disturbed areas, rocky places, coastal beaches, arid grasslands, and flood plains, where it is growing as isolated patches, maybe up to 70 individual in each population ( Nattero and Cocucci, 2007).
4: N. glauca grows in a wide range of elevations and soil conditions that enabling it to grow vigorously and spread as a monospecific stand ( Florentine et al., 2006,  Thomas et al., 2016,  Al-Robai et al., 2019).
5: N. glauca is a reservoir plant of dangerous viruses that infect many plant species including cultivated plants such as tobacco mosaic virus (TMV) and tomato infectious chlorosis crinivirus (TICV) that have been discovered in  N. glauca, as host, in several parts of the world ( Duffus et al., 1996,  Favara et al., 2019).
6: Also, this plant has been reported to produce anabasine which is a poisonous chemical compound to both human and animals ( Scharenberg et al., 2019).
