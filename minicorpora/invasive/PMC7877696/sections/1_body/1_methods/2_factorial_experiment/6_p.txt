0: Once  Podophyllum peltatum began to senesce in the field in mid-July 2014, the pots containing the  Podophyllum peltatum rhizomes were stored in an uninsulated barn during the winter to prevent the rhizomes from freezing and dying.
1: The pots were then pulled out of storage in early April 2015.
2: Sixteen worms were again added to  Lumbricus terrestris present pots, and 100 g of  Alliaria petiolata was again added weekly to  Alliaria petiolata present pots in the summer of 2015 until 12 June, when the  Podophyllum peltatum plants were harvested.
3: Roots were washed and biomass was dried in the drying oven for 24 h (leaves) or 72 h (stems, rhizomes, roots and reproductive) at 60 C, and weighed to 0.01 g.
