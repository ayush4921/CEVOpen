0: The study was conducted along the entire stretch of the Kenya Standard Gauge Railway (SGR) Phase I & Phase IIA, covering eight counties from Mombasa to Narok ( Fig 1).
1: The SGR has been billed as the biggest transport infrastructure in the countrys history under the Vision 2030 development agenda [ 34].
2: The SGR forms part of the East African Railway Master Plan (2009) and the Eastern African SGR regional network which aims to rejuvenate existing railways serving Tanzania, Kenya, Uganda and extend to Burundi, Rwanda, Ethiopia and South Sudan [ 35].
3: The SGR runs westwards from the coastal town of Mombasa and through the central Kenya with the line through western Kenya to Malaba town at the Kenya-Uganda border still under construction to link up with other standard gauge railways that are being built in East Africa [ 35].
4: The construction of the SGR begun in 2014 at an estimated cost of US$3.8 billion, with 90% supplied by a loan from the Exim Bank of China and 10% coming from the Kenyan government [ 34].
